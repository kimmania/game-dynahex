How to Play Dynahex

Dynahex is a logic puzzle like Hexcells or Minesweeper, but the board moves. Every few turns, unanchored cells slide to new positions and clue numbers change.

Your Goal

The black clue hexes show a number — that's how many of their adjacent hexes are "true" (must be marked orange). Every non-clue hex is either true or safe. Use the clues to figure out which is which.

Mark true hexes orange · Clear safe hexes blue · Solve every hex to win

Controls

  • Tap an unknown cell to cycle: → Marked → Cleared → back to unknown. Clue hexes are given — you can't change them.
  • Spend an anchor to lock a cell in place so it won't drift. Limited supply — use wisely. An anchored cell gets a cyan rim.
  • Preview where cells will drift on the next tick. Ghost arrows show movement; red glow means a cell will be lost off the board edge.

Drift

Every N moves (shown in the goal banner at the top of the board), all unanchored unknown cells slide to an adjacent empty spot. After drifting, clue numbers recount based on the new neighbor layout.

Marked and Cleared cells never drift. Anchored cells never drift. Only unknown cells move.

Winning

Resolve every hex on the board — mark all true hexes orange and clear all safe hexes blue. When every cell is correctly resolved, you win!

If cells drift off the board, the puzzle gets harder but may still be solvable. Use Foresight and anchors to prevent losses.
