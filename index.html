How to Play Dynahex

Dynahex is a hex-grid logic puzzle where the board itself is unstable. Cells physically drift across the grid every few moves, rewriting adjacency relationships and invalidating your deductions.

Goal

Mark every true hex (orange) and clear every safe hex (blue). The level is solved when all cells are resolved and the grid survives a full drift tick with zero movement.

Cell States

  • Unknown — Unresolved; may hold a clue or be empty. Drifts if unanchored.
  • Marked — Confirmed as part of the solution (orange). Does not drift.
  • Cleared — Confirmed safe (blue). Does not drift.
  • 3 Clue — Shows how many adjacent hexes are true. Drifts if unanchored.
  • Anchored — Locked in place (cyan rim). Costs 1 anchor.
  • Stale — Was correct, but its validating clue drifted away (cosmetic only).

Controls

  • Mark mode (default): Tap an unknown cell to cycle Marked → Cleared → Unknown.
  • Anchor mode: Tap any cell to spend 1 anchor and lock it in place. Anchors are limited and don't regenerate.
  • Foresight: Tap to preview where all unanchored cells will drift on the next tick. Ghost arrows show drift targets; red glow indicates cells that will be lost off the board.

Drift

Every N moves (shown in the status bar), all unanchored unknown cells drift to an adjacent empty position using a deterministic seeded order. After drifting, clue numbers update to reflect the new neighbor layout. Resolved and anchored cells never drift.

Scoring (✓/🔒)

  • ✓ Stabilized — All cells resolved; grid survived a drift tick with zero movement.
  • ✓ Conservation — Used ≤50% of available anchors.
  • ✓ No Losses — Zero cells drifted off the board edge.
  • ✓ Foresight — Solved without any cells entering Stale status.
  • 🔒 Speedrun — Under par time for the grid size.
